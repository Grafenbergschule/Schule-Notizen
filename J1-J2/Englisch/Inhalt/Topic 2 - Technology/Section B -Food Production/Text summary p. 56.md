### **Goals and how the company will reach them**

- **Primary Goal:** To "eradicate" the meat and fish industries by **2035**.
    
- **Environmental Mission:** Stop the global collapse of biodiversity and turn back the clock on climate change.
    
- **Land Recovery:** Return the **45%** of Earth’s land currently used for animal agriculture to nature.
    
- **The Method:** Replace animals as a "food technology" by using scientists and food technicians to create plant-based substitutes for every animal product.
    
- **Scientific Approach:** "Cracking meat’s molecular code" to replicate the texture, taste, and sensation of meat using plant-based ingredients like **heme**.
    

### **Founding, investments, products, sales**

- **Founding:** Started in **2011** by Patrick Brown, a former Stanford University biochemistry professor, following a sabbatical researching intensive farming.
    
- **Investments:** Has attracted nearly **$1.3 billion** in investment; notable investors include Jay-Z, Katy Perry, Serena Williams, and Trevor Noah.
    
- **Products:** The flagship "Impossible Burger," Impossible Pork, and Impossible Sausage. Milk and fish equivalents are currently in development.
    
- **Sales:** Available in thousands of restaurants (including 7,000 Burger Kings) across the US, Hong Kong, and Singapore; also sold in supermarkets.
    

### **Challenges and controversies**

- **Cost:** Products are currently expensive (e.g., $65 for a 5lb pack of ground beef), though the company aims to lower costs as it grows.
    
- **Consumer Preference:** A 2020 survey found that over two-thirds of US adults still identify as meat eaters and rate beef burgers higher for taste.
    
- **Health/Safety Concerns:** Faced criticism from organizations (e.g., Friends of the Earth) over the use of **genetically modified ingredients** and whether "heme" has been properly tested.
    
- **"Processed" Label:** Critics describe the products as "overprocessed." Brown counters this by comparing the process to baking bread and arguing the ingredients are "thoughtfully put together."
