### Adam's Appearance

- **Physique:** Compactly built, square-shouldered, and handsome.
    
- **Features:** Narrow face, dark skin (acquires a "rich human colour" when charged), thick black hair, and hooded eyes.
    
- **Texture:** Warm, lifelike skin that is as smooth as a child's.
    

### What He Can Do

- **Chores:** Wash dishes and make beds.
    
- **Intellect:** Act as a friend, "think," and record/retrieve everything he sees and hears.
    
- **Stamina:** Run 17 km in 2 hours or operate non-stop for 12 days.
    

### What He Can't Do

- **Vehicles:** Drive a car.
    
- **Water:** Swim, shower, or go in the rain without an umbrella.
    
- **Hazards:** Operate a chainsaw without supervision.

  ### 1. The first truly viable manufactured human with plausible intelligence and looks... (ll. 3–4)

- **Mögliche Umschreibung:** The very first functional artificial human who actually looked and thought like a real person...
    
- _Oder kürzer:_ The first successful human-like android that had believable features and a realistic mind...
    

### 2. Twelve of this first edition were called Adam, thirteen were called Eve. Corny, everyone agreed, but commercial. (ll. 10–11)

- **Mögliche Umschreibung:** Out of the initial batch, twelve male models were named Adam and thirteen female models were named Eve. Although everyone found these names cliché, they were chosen for marketing reasons.
    
- _Oder kürzer:_ The first twenty-five units were named Adam and Eve. People thought it was cheesy, but it made sense for sales.

### 3. Notions of biological race being scientifically discredited, the twenty-five were designed to cover a range of ethnicities. (ll. 11–12)

- **Mögliche Umschreibung:** Because science has proven that biological races do not exist, the twenty-five models were created to represent diverse cultural and ethnic backgrounds.
    
- _Oder kürzer:_ Since the concept of race is scientifically invalid, the androids were built with a variety of global appearances.
    

### 4. It would take sixteen hours to fire him up. (ll. 26–27)

- **Mögliche Umschreibung:** It required sixteen hours to fully turn him on and start his system.
    
- _Oder kürzer:_ Starting the android up for the first time took sixteen hours.
    

### 5. We knew from the excited publicity that he formed sounds with breath, tongue, teeth and palate. (ll. 30–31)

- **Mögliche Umschreibung:** According to the enthusiastic advertisements, we were aware that his speech was produced using realistic human vocal mechanics like breathing and mouth movements.
    
- _Oder kürzer:_ The promotions had hyped up the fact that he spoke just like a human, using his mouth and breath.

nö

### 6. I thought that for the sum I'd handed over after lunch, Adam should have been charged up and ready to go. (ll. 51–52)

- **Mögliche Umschreibung:** I felt that considering the huge amount of money I paid earlier that afternoon, the android should have arrived fully powered and operational.
    
- _Oder kürzer:_ For the price I paid after lunch, I expected Adam to be completely charged and working immediately.
